1. Inspiration

In 2016, 47000 seniors lived alone. By 2030, 83000 elderly will live alone. In 2019 a voice mentioned neighbours are important. But yet, how many people actually do we go out and speak to? No system has been established.

Then, in 2020, Singapore experienced the serious COVID-19 pandemic. Due to circuit breaking measures and prevent further spread of the virus, Singaporeans were told not to visit their grandparents. Old age homes were also closed due to a big cluster, to advocate prevention for a further spread of the virus.

Circuit breaking measures have since been released. However, no one can predict what will happen next. We never want such lonely situations to happen again. So, we need to prevent this. And thus, Lim Kopi, a system for seniors, or anyone really, to talk share and learn together, was born.

2. Idea

Lim Kopi will be a system, suitable for elders, or any adult really. It's more of an open course / sharing programme, grouped based on physical locations, based on WhatsApp. We will split users up into group chats based on physical locations (North, West and East to start of with and expanding), then have volunteers in each group chat to facilitate courses and conversations. Once people are used to each other, they can start hosting meetings on their own, sharing their skills and experiences, etc. It's a rather open concept, just that we get as many people up to join the conversation.

These are details of how it will work. We spread the message out; seniors who might just want to find someone to have a conversation can just simply message our Lim Kopi Admin. Following that, the contact will be passed to volunteers (who have been vetted), and then after having a simple video/voice call over WhatsApp for introduction and security purposes, he/she will be able to join the group chat.

In the group chat, volunteers will host own meetings and activities. These can be online or offline. They can do what they are good at, e.g. art, cooking, exercises, introduction to technology etc. Seniors themselves can also share what they enjoy doing. The main point of this is to allow both seniors and volunteers to come together in an open environment, so everyone is cared for through cultivating a kampung spirit.

Here's how our system sets us apart from typical old age homes, and why it works:

  • It's free.
  • It just happens at home virtually, or in near areas in a physical community.
    (Users are tagged location-based).
  • Anyone can learn from each other, with emotional support.
  • It cultivates a community spirit, so in case anything happens in future,
    we have someone to rely on/talk to.
  • It is simple to use. Simply WhatsApp, which many seniors should know.
  • Our system is safe with vetting put into place.
  • We are constantly expanding to cultivate more like-minded conversations and activities.
  • There is literally NO online system like this, although it is rather simple to do honestly.
  • There will be so many people to talk to, if everyone joins the database. Surely not a dull conversation.
  • Learn from the comfort of your home, but still with accompaniment.

Thus, we have everyone in the community learning together and from each other.

Our system is launching on August 1. Before that ,we just want to call out for volunteers, seniors, anyone at all to join and participate. We'll collate a list. On August 1, we'll start off with a range of activities and conversations. Simple as that. We want to get everybody on board and make sure they won't miss anything. So we're launching, on 2020, August 1.

I wish to take this forward, as far as I can, and establish the Kampung spirit that holds us Singaporeans united. In case any pandemic resurfaces, we can then stand firmly together. And even if none of these happen in the future, we have a free-styled community where volunteers gear up and do whatever they want, and if each person does his/her part in the community, then nobody will be left alone again.
